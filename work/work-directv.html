Real-Time OOH Hijack

DIRECTV needed to remind cord-cutters that live TV still matters—especially for cultural tentpole moments. With the Emmy Awards approaching, we saw an opportunity to turn Hollywood's biggest night into a brand moment.

The Insight

The Emmys celebrate the best in television. But to truly experience them, you need to watch live. The second-screen experience of Twitter reactions and spoilers doesn't compare to seeing the moment as it happens.

DIRECTV could own that message—in the most visible way possible.

The Execution

We secured premium OOH placements throughout Hollywood—billboards along the Emmy red carpet route, digital screens in key entertainment districts, and wraps on buildings visible from the Peacock Theatre.

The twist: our creative was updated in real-time as winners were announced. "Congrats, Bear" appeared minutes after Jeremy Allen White won. "History Made" ran the moment Shogun swept. Every update reinforced the message: this is what you miss when you're not watching live.

My Role

As Senior Connections Strategist at TBWA\Chiat\Day LA, I led the connections orchestration for the Emmys Hijack from concept through execution. I scouted and mapped high-impact OOH sites and projection surfaces throughout Hollywood, identifying the locations that would create maximum visibility during the award show. I developed the media sequencing that allowed our creative to update in real-time as winners were announced—a technical and logistical challenge that required close coordination with our OOH partners.

I authored the briefs, coordinated rapid client approvals, and served as the connective tissue between Creative, PR, and Media to ensure every touchpoint reinforced the same message: live TV still matters. I also identified the Rotten Tomatoes homepage takeover opportunity that extended our reach to entertainment enthusiasts exploring post-Emmy buzz. The whole campaign was a masterclass in agile marketing—and proof that DIRECTV owns live cultural moments.

DIRECTV Like You've Never Seen It

DIRECTV was undergoing a transformation—from satellite provider to streaming-first platform. This campaign introduced the new DIRECTV to a world that thought they already knew the brand.

The Insight

People had strong memories of DIRECTV—the satellite dish, the installation, the commitment. But they didn't know about the new DIRECTV: streaming, flexibility, no dish required. We needed to reset expectations entirely.

The Execution

A campaign that leaned into the transformation with humor and celebrity power. We showed viewers that this wasn't their parents' DIRECTV—it was something entirely new, built for how people watch today.

Multiple spots highlighted different aspects of the new experience: the content, the flexibility, the sports, and the simplicity.

My Role

As Senior Connections Strategist, I helped develop the media approach that reintroduced DIRECTV to cord-cutters and cord-nevers. I identified the key audience segments who held outdated perceptions of the brand and mapped the media environments where we could intercept them with our repositioning message.

I contributed to the channel mix strategy across connected TV, streaming, social, and digital video—ensuring our celebrity-driven spots appeared in contexts that reinforced the "new DIRECTV" message. I also developed competitive conquesting recommendations that allowed us to reach audiences actively considering other streaming options. The campaign drove a 41% lift in brand awareness and became the most recalled campaign in category tracking.

Nothing On Your Roof 2.0

The sequel to a successful campaign that made one thing crystal clear: you can get all of DIRECTV's premium content without a satellite dish. Period.

The Insight

The biggest barrier to DIRECTV consideration was still the dish. People assumed they'd need one. They assumed installation headaches. They assumed commitment. All wrong.

We needed to hammer this message home one more time—with even more impact.

The Execution

Building on the success of the original "Nothing On Your Roof" campaign, 2.0 took the message further with new talent, new scenarios, and new humor. The simplicity of the message—no dish required—remained the hero.

My Role

As Senior Connections Strategist, I developed the media strategy to hammer home DIRECTV's "no dish" message to the audiences who still didn't believe it. I analyzed brand tracking data to identify the demographic segments with the strongest dish perception barrier and built targeting strategies to reach them efficiently.

I recommended placement strategies that put our message in contexts where the old DIRECTV associations were strongest—during live sports, appointment TV, and in-home moments when viewers might be thinking about their own rooftops. The result was a 34% reduction in the dish perception barrier and a meaningful lift in brand consideration.
