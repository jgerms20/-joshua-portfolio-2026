No Ordinary Athlete

Gatorade owns sports. But to stay relevant with younger consumers, the brand needed to expand its cultural footprint beyond the playing field. With Stranger Things Season 5 approaching, we saw an opportunity to connect athletic performance with one of the most-watched shows on the planet—and celebrate the brand's authentic 1980s heritage by reviving iconic products and ads from the vault.

The campaign perfectly captured the retro Hawkins vibe full of neon, nostalgia, and the supernatural Upside Down. We gave a vintage jingle new life in Stranger Things' Upside Down, with "No Ordinary Athlete" as a play on the brand's iconic "No Ordinary Thirst Quencher" campaign from the '80s.

The Insight

Stranger Things' heroes aren't typical athletes—but they're athletes nonetheless. Eleven's psychic powers require intense mental and physical exertion. The kids spend the entire series running, fighting, and pushing their limits. They're performing at their peak when it matters most.

That's exactly what Gatorade fuels. Narrated by Myles Garrett—defensive end for the Cleveland Browns and Stranger Things super-fan—the campaign shows the role Gatorade plays in both real-world performance and the Upside Down, where they're hoping to save the world.

"Grab your scrunchies and tube socks because Gatorade is going full-on '80s in collaboration with Stranger Things ahead of its fifth and final season. The collab — complete with the return of the discontinued Citrus Cooler flavor and two vintage-inspired merch capsules — perfectly captures the retro Hawkins vibe full of neon, nostalgia, and the supernatural Upside Down."
— Lyda Cosgrove, Her Campus
"No Ordinary Athlete."
— Campaign Tagline

The Execution

We developed limited-edition Stranger Things Gatorade bottles featuring the Upside Down aesthetic—flickering lights, demogorgon shadows, and that signature red-and-black color palette. The packaging became a collector's item, with the return of glass bottles becoming a centerpiece of the campaign.

The campaign extended across digital, social, TV, OOH, and in-store, with content that positioned the Stranger Things cast as athletes performing extraordinary feats. We created "training" content showing the kids preparing for their battles—all fueled by Gatorade. The phased rollout kept fans engaged with every drop, analyzing our content for potential easter eggs and connections to the show, making the brand an essential part of the Stranger Things universe.

We also brought back the discontinued Citrus Cooler flavor as a Limited Time Offering (LTO), tapping into nostalgia and re-engaging previously interested media who amplified the news across food & beverage outlets.

"The [glass bottle] comeback is a centerpiece of Gatorade's "No Ordinary Athlete" campaign, a modern revival of its classic 1987 "No Ordinary Thirst Quencher" ads. With an Upside Down twist, the campaign merges sports culture, retro design, and Stranger Things nostalgia."
— Shawn Grant, The Source

My Role

As Senior Connections Strategist at TBWA\Chiat\Day LA, I led the connections strategy that brought this nostalgic partnership to life. I developed the channel approach that maximized the Netflix partnership—strategically timing our content to run during Netflix's historic Christmas Day NFL games, a moment where sports fans and Stranger Things enthusiasts overlapped perfectly.

I orchestrated the coordination between our paid media flights and the limited-edition merchandise launches (the 1987 Hawkins Capsule and Upside Down Capsule), ensuring fans encountered a cohesive experience across every touchpoint. I worked closely with our PR team to drive compelling storytelling across priority marketing and trade outlets, leveraging the campaign's nostalgia and pop-culture relevance to secure coverage from outlets like Marketing Dive and Food & Beverage Magazine.

I also identified social amplification opportunities with cast members and developed the influencer seeding strategy that got the collectible bottles into the hands of superfans who would organically spread the word. By engaging Stranger Things superfans across sports and culture media, we seeded the capsule drops timed to episode airings, with social amplification from Megan Behnke (PopCulture) and Rebecca Lobo (ESPN).

The partnership became a case study in how entertainment collabs can extend a sports brand's cultural relevance—driving the strongest positive sentiment and largest volume of mentions across similar Stranger Things partnerships.

Earned Media & PR

Our PR approach drove compelling storytelling across priority marketing and trade outlets beyond the TVC by leveraging the campaign's nostalgia and pop-culture relevance. This resulted in coverage from outlets such as Marketing Dive and Food & Beverage Magazine without direct executive access.

Conversation Themes

Phased rollout kept fans engaged: Releasing our content in phases, beginning with a teaser before Volume 1 dropped, had fans hungry for each drop after, analyzing our content for potential easter eggs and connections to the show, making the brand an essential part of the Stranger Things universe.

Authentic 80s branding induced nostalgia: Fans of all ages showed a feverish desire to get their hands on the glass bottles. Older fans reminisced on the products that remind them of their youth, while younger fans wanted to get a taste of the retro aesthetic.

Exclusive drops sustained momentum: The limited-edition nature of the capsules created urgency and FOMO, driving record traffic and sign-ups. The Hawkins Drop saw the single highest hour of traffic ever recorded on Gatorade.com with 22.5K sessions hitting the site between 9-10am CT.

The Impact

The partnership became one of the most successful entertainment collaborations in Gatorade's history. Limited-edition bottles sold out within days. The campaign drove significant conversation among Gen Z consumers who saw Gatorade as more than just a sports drink—it became a cultural artifact.

Most importantly, it proved that Gatorade's "fuel the best" positioning could extend beyond traditional athletics into any arena where performance matters. The campaign's nostalgia-led storytelling and athlete interviews drove cross-cultural conversation, while exclusive drops and the glass bottle return sustained campaign momentum and cultural buzz.

Posts featuring Gatorade appeared across the Stranger Things & Myles Garrett social accounts, bringing in millions more views from superfans. The 'El's Battery' Video alone was seen almost 6 million times on Instagram, with high engagement on TikTok including almost 10k shares.

All The Reasons Not to Quit

In partnership with NFL Films, we told the story of resilience, determination, and the will to keep going when everything says you should stop. This campaign focused on the teams and players who embody the never-give-up spirit that Gatorade has always championed.

The Insight

There's a team that lost for 10 years straight. Season after season of heartbreak. But they never stopped showing up. They never stopped believing. They never stopped fighting for that next win.

Their story is Gatorade's story: fuel the fight, not just the victory.

"The hardest thing isn't winning. It's deciding to keep going when you haven't."
— Campaign Insight

The Execution

Working closely with NFL Films, we crafted a documentary-style campaign that followed players through their lowest moments—and their comeback. The cinematic quality brought authenticity and emotion that typical sports marketing lacks.

The partnership extended beyond advertising into content, with behind-the-scenes features, player profiles, and real stories of perseverance that resonated across platforms.

My Role

As Senior Connections Strategist, I developed the distribution strategy for this powerful documentary series. I mapped out the rollout cadence, identifying the platforms and cultural moments that would maximize emotional resonance—from social media teasers that built anticipation to the Today Show coverage that brought the story to mainstream audiences.

I partnered closely with our PR team to craft a narrative arc for the campaign's earned media, pitching exclusive angles to sports and culture outlets. I also identified community partnership opportunities that extended the campaign's message of perseverance beyond traditional advertising—connecting with schools, youth sports organizations, and coaching communities who could champion the "never quit" ethos. The work reminded me why I love what I do: using media strategy to amplify stories that actually matter.
