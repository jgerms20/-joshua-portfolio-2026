Don't Overthink It

Holiday gifting is stressful. Everyone wants to find the perfect gift, but the pressure to get it exactly right can paralyze shoppers. Sephora's Holiday 2024 campaign tackled that anxiety head-on with a simple message: Don't Overthink It.

The Insight

Beauty is deeply personal, which makes gifting it feel risky. What if they don't like the shade? What if it's the wrong product for their skin? But here's the truth: when you gift from Sephora, you're not just giving a product—you're giving an experience, a moment of self-care, an invitation to explore.

The gift itself matters less than the intention behind it.

The Execution

Taika Waititi brought his signature humor and warmth to four holiday spots that celebrated the chaos, the last-minute panic, and the joy of giving. The campaign featured real moments of holiday stress turned into moments of connection—all anchored by the reassurance that Sephora has you covered.

The multi-platform campaign included TV, digital, social, and in-store activations that reinforced the "Don't Overthink It" message at every touchpoint.

My Role

As Senior Connections Strategist at TBWA\Chiat\Day LA, I crafted the holistic media strategy that brought Taika Waititi's vision to screens across the country. I led the channel planning process, identifying the optimal mix of connected TV, streaming platforms, and social to reach stressed holiday shoppers at exactly the right moments—when they were feeling that gift-giving anxiety most acutely.

I built the campaign's rollout calendar, sequencing content drops to maintain momentum from Thanksgiving through Christmas Eve. I partnered with our media agency to negotiate premium placements during key viewing moments and developed the social amplification strategy that extended our reach beyond paid media. We worked as a team to ensure every touchpoint reinforced the "Don't Overthink It" message, and I'm proud that our work helped earn recognition as one of AdWeek's Best Holiday Ads of 2024.

The Impact

The "Don't Overthink It" campaign became one of Sephora's most successful holiday efforts, driving record sales while reinforcing the brand's position as the go-to destination for beauty gifting. The humorous approach cut through the noise of traditional holiday advertising and resonated with stressed shoppers who just wanted permission to trust their instincts.

Skin Obsessed

Skincare has become one of the most passionate categories in beauty. The "Skin Obsessed" campaign celebrated the devoted skincare community—the ingredient nerds, the routine builders, the before-and-after documenters—and positioned Sephora as their ultimate destination.

The Insight

Skincare enthusiasts aren't just consumers—they're researchers, experimenters, and evangelists. They read ingredient lists like wine labels, track their progress with clinical precision, and spread the gospel of their holy grail products with religious fervor.

They're obsessed. And that obsession is beautiful.

The Execution

Again partnering with Taika Waititi, we created spots that celebrated skincare obsession in all its glory—from the elaborate 12-step routines to the late-night ingredient deep-dives. The campaign was playful but affirming: being obsessed with your skin isn't vanity, it's self-care.

The campaign launched with hero spots and extended into social content, influencer partnerships, and in-store activations that invited customers to embrace their inner skincare nerd.

My Role

I led the connections strategy for "Skin Obsessed," diving deep into the skincare enthusiast mindset to understand where and how to reach them. I mapped out audience journeys across connected TV, online video, paid social, and streaming audio—identifying the platforms where ingredient nerds spend their time researching their next holy grail product.

I curated influencer partnership recommendations, focusing on authentic skincare voices who could extend our campaign's reach within the community. Working with our creative team, I ensured the media placements matched the campaign's playful energy—placing spots during beauty-adjacent content and building a retargeting strategy that caught interested shoppers at the consideration moment. Our work positions Sephora to capture share in the booming global skincare market projected to exceed $194 billion by 2032.
