About Me

Born and raised in South Carolina, I’ve always been a curious cat. Whether it’s a book in my hand, a podcast in my ears, or a camera at my side, I’ve always taken joy in learning more about the world around me and what makes it go round.

This curiosity has guided me through every stage of my life; from being an active member of the newspaper club in high school, to studying Journalism & Mass Communications at the University of South Carolina, where I minored in Information Science and served as assistant sports editor for The Daily Gamecock. It eventually led me into the world of advertising, where I’ve built a career in Strategy at some of the top agencies in the United States.

Now, living in Los Angeles, California, my curiosity fuels each of my creative pursuits: photography, podcasting, creative writing, movies & TV and building with Artificial Intelligence. I’m drawn to authentic storytelling that captures the present moment while honoring the past and building the future.

Brand Work / Levi's REIIMAGINE

Levi's x Beyoncé REIIMAGINE Campaign

Role Lead Strategic Planner
Agency TBWA\Chiat\Day
Year 2024-2025

Campaign Overview

The Levi's® REIIMAGINE campaign with Beyoncé is a bold celebration of the brand's legacy as a canvas for self-expression, reimagining iconic ads for a modern era. Inspired by Beyoncé's track "LEVII'S JEANS" from her critically acclaimed album COWBOY CARTER, the campaign unfolds in chapters, each reinterpreting a classic Levi's® advertisement. I led the strategy at TBWA\Chiat\Day, focusing on connecting with fans through a global, multi-channel approach. The campaign sparked $65M+ in earned media within 48 hours, proving Levi's® remains the definitive denim lifestyle brand.

"No Ordinary Athlete" — Stranger Things Collaboration

Gatorade teamed up with Netflix to give a vintage jingle new life in Stranger Things' Upside Down. "No Ordinary Athlete" is a play on the brand's iconic "No Ordinary Thirst Quencher" campaign from the '80s. Narrated by Myles Garrett—defensive end for the Cleveland Browns and Stranger Things super-fan—the :30 spot from TBWA\Chiat\Day L.A. shows the role Gatorade plays in both real-world performance and the Upside Down, where they're hoping to save the world.

The campaign celebrates Gatorade's authentic 1980s heritage by reviving iconic products and ads from the vault, pulling them into the 'Upside Down.' When the final season of Stranger Things dropped, the brand launched a 1987 Hawkins Capsule and Upside Down Capsule, with vintage apparel, retro Gatorade towels and more.

Campaign Details

For over 60 years, Gatorade, the OG sports drink, has fueled the world's greatest athletes. Now in 1987 Hawkins, the crew is gearing up for the final battle with Gatorade—choosing no ordinary thirst quencher to fuel the extraordinary. The collaboration celebrates our authentic 1980s heritage by reviving iconic products and ads from our vault and pulling them into the 'Upside Down.'

The work is running on social and digital and during Netflix's Christmas Day NFL games. When the final season of Stranger Things dropped, the brand launched a 1987 Hawkins Capsule and Upside Down Capsule, with vintage apparel, retro Gatorade towels and more.

"All the Reasons Not to Quit" — Columbia High School Football

Gatorade's powerful campaign celebrating resilience and determination through the story of Columbia High School's football team. After losing for 10 years straight, the team's unwavering commitment to showing up, working hard, and never giving up embodies what it truly means to be an athlete—not just when you win, but especially when you don't.

This campaign from TBWA\Chiat\Day L.A. highlights the real stories of perseverance that happen off the field, celebrating the moments that define character and grit. It's about finding strength in struggle, unity in adversity, and the unbreakable spirit that keeps athletes coming back, game after game, season after season.

Campaign Details

"All the Reasons Not to Quit" tells the authentic story of Columbia High School's football team—a team that faced 10 consecutive losing seasons but never stopped showing up. This campaign celebrates what it truly means to be an athlete: the commitment, the work ethic, the camaraderie, and the unshakeable determination that exists beyond the scoreboard.

Emmys OOH Hijack — Awards Night Takeover

A real‑time Emmys takeover built around DIRECTV’s Universal Search tool. We placed DIRECTV’s search UI alongside award‑season IP — from Andor and Only Murders in the Building to The White Lotus, The Penguin and The Pitt — to make a simple point at a maximal moment: open DIRECTV, type what you want, and watch across partners without the hunt.

I led connections orchestration: mapping high‑impact sites and projection surfaces, sequencing flights directly after the Emmys broadcast, and aligning paid/organic social with our OOH creative. I authored briefs, coordinated rapid approvals, and synced Creative, PR and Media so every touchpoint reinforced the same product truth. We extended the stunt with a Rotten Tomatoes homepage takeover to capture curiosity and drive exploration.

The activation ran across Hollywood, West Hollywood, Santa Monica and Downtown LA in the days surrounding the show — celebrating winners while reminding people where to find the most talked‑about series. Channels spanned print OOH, digital OOH, projections and social; content was captured and published in‑flight.
